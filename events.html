Slow Money Austin Showcase Conference -

Austin City Hall

April 21

This April, Slow Money Austin brings the slow money movement to Central Texas, spotlighting the regional food system and its shift toward sustainability. This gathering, which brings together forward-looking activists, producers, purveyors, entrepreneurs, investors and political leaders, will feature:

- a keynote speech by noted venture capitalist and social investor Woody Tasch, author of Inquiries into the Nature of Slow Money

- presentations highlighting the social and economic importance of the region's emerging local food movement

- showcases profiling key community leaders, and regional and organic food system businesses

- panel discussions exploring the challenges and opportunities facing participants in the food-to-table-to-food chain

Slow Money Austin Fundraising Dinner by Dai Due Supper Club

Barr Mansion

April 22, 6:30pm

No other culinary artisans prove as active or talented advocates of the slow food movement than Jesse Griffiths and Tamara Mayfield, whose acclaimed Dai Due Supper Club marries local food awareness with award-winning social and culinary experiences. This special dinner, held at famed culinary destination Barr Mansion, promises to provide a delectable exploration of the remarkably diverse, sustainably grown riches Central Texas has to offer.
